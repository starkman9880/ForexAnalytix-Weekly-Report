Weekly Report (March 13th - 17th 2023)

Another eventful week came to a close, with market uncertainty and volatility at high levels. The banking crisis that started with Silicon Valley Bank in the US seems to be gripping the rest of the US regional baking sector. Meanwhile, the Swiss National Bank stepped in to help Credit Suisse, whose situation remains very fragile. In the midst of all this the ECB raised rates by 50bps, choosing to not allow the situation to move them away from their goal of fighting inflation.

The US Dollar remained under pressure during the whole week as US yields collapsed and US economic data came in weak. The DXY index fell 0.7% to close at 103.864.
 
The Euro also fell last week against most major currencies, as the ECB’s 50bp hike was more than offset by the European banking sector’s worries. The single currency fell 1% against the GBP and around 2% vs. the Yen, AUD and NZD.
 
The GBP had a mixed week in the absence of any meaningful UK data or local developments.
Commodity currencies did well as yields fell and the US Dollar retreated lower. The CAD rallied 0.7% against the USD, while the AUD and NZD gained around 2%. The NOK was the week’s underperformer with a 0.8% fall. Elsewhere in FX, the CHF dropped 0.5%, the MXN fell over 2% and the week’s best performer was the JPY with a 2.4% rally against the Dollar.
 
Oil is in trouble as the global economic environment looks fragile. The banking crisis is bringing great uncertainty and as a result oil trades very heavy. Last week the WTI collapsed over 13% to close at $66.22.
 
Precious metals had an excellent week as money flowed into “safe haven” trades. Is this their time to shine? Time will tell, but technically Gold has broken out of resistance and is heading to new highs. Last week Gold rallied over 6% to $1,988 and Silver gained over 10% so close at $22.60.

Equities are still trading very well, given the general environment. The banking sector is underperforming but overall, the major indices are once again characterised by resilience. Last week the S&P500 index closed 1.4% higher at 3914 points, while the DAX fell 3.7% to 14,770 points.
 
Bonds were once again one of the main “safe haven” asset classes, registering another very strong week. The 10y UST yield fell 30bps to 3.40%, and the 10y Bund rallied 3.2% to close at 137.983 points.
 
Finally, crypto-currencies saw another wave of buying as the banking crisis fear gripped many asset classes. At the time of writing Bitcoin is over 30% higher at $27,000 and Ethereum is rallying over 20% at $1,800.
The Week Ahead:

Another busy week is ahead of us, with the big question mark being the banking sector situation. Will European banks get dragged into the mess? Will more central banks get involved in order to restore confidence?
 
We have the Federal Reserve interest rate decision and a 25bp hike is expected – will the Fed surprise the markets? We also have rate decisions from the SNB and BoE, as well as a broad number of PMI releases. Good luck!
